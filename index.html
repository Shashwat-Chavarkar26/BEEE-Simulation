How it Works

This simulation demonstrates an Operational Amplifier (Op-Amp) acting as a Voltage Comparator. Unlike a standard amplifier that scales a signal, a comparator evaluates which of its two inputs is higher and "snaps" its output to the maximum possible voltage (Saturation).

Non-Inverting Mode:
If Vin (+) > Vref (-), then Vout = Vcc (High)
If Vin (+) < Vref (-), then Vout = 0V (Low)

In Inverting Mode, the logic is flipped because the signal is compared at the negative terminal. This makes the Op-Amp a basic 1-bit Analog-to-Digital Converter (ADC).

Key Concept: Saturation

Because Op-Amps have extremely high "Open-Loop Gain" (often > 100,000x), even a tiny difference of 0.001V between the inputs would result in a theoretical output of 100V. Since the Op-Amp cannot output more than its supply, it Saturates at Vcc (5V in this demo).
